Program 01

🌾 Sustainable Agriculture & Agri-Technology

The Kamba heartland of Machakos, Kitui, and Makueni faces one of Kenya's most severe drought challenges. ADADO's agriculture program tackles food insecurity head-on through modern, climate-smart farming practices that work with the environment, not against it.

Rainwater Harvesting & Irrigation

Training farmers in efficient water management systems including sand dams, zai pits, and drip irrigation.

Drought-Resistant Crop Varieties

Introducing and distributing seeds for heat-tolerant crops — cowpeas, sorghum, millet — suited for arid regions.

Digital Market Access

Connecting farmers directly to buyers through mobile platforms — cutting out middlemen and increasing farmer income.

Livestock Development

Supporting sustainable goat, cattle, and poultry farming with veterinary training and market linkages.

"When one Kamba farmer thrives, the whole community eats. Our agriculture program is about dignity, food security, and self-sufficiency for every Kamba household."

— ADADO Agriculture Director

Program 02

🎓 Centers of Excellence & Educational Upliftment

Education is the most powerful tool for generational change. ADADO is establishing Centers of Excellence — purpose-built hubs that combine vocational training, entrepreneurship education, and academic support for Kamba youth in Kenya and North Carolina.

🏫
Vocational Training

Practical skills in carpentry, technology, tailoring, agribusiness, and more.

📚
Scholarship Programs

Financial support for Kamba youth in North Carolina and Kenya pursuing higher education.

💻
Digital Literacy

Computer training, coding bootcamps, and internet literacy for rural communities.

🚀
Entrepreneurship

Business skills, microfinance access, and mentorship for Kamba entrepreneurs.

Program 03

🤝 Civic Engagement & Diaspora Responsibility

We believe that every Kamba in the diaspora has a responsibility — and an opportunity — to contribute to community development back home and in North Carolina.

Diaspora Mentorship

Connecting successful Kamba professionals in the U.S. with youth in Kenya through structured mentorship programs — sharing skills, knowledge, and life experience.

Civic Education

Empowering Kamba community members in North Carolina with civic rights, voter registration guidance, and community advocacy training.

Youth Leadership

Identifying and developing the next generation of Kamba community leaders through structured leadership programs in the U.S. and Kenya.

Program 04

💧 Food Security & Water Conservation

The counties of Machakos, Kitui, and Makueni face severe water scarcity — a challenge that threatens livelihoods, health, and food security for millions. ADADO's water program works to change this reality permanently.

🏗️ Sand Dam Construction

Funding community-built sand dams that capture rainwater in riverbeds, recharging water tables and providing year-round access to clean water.

🌦️ Climate Adaptation

Training communities in climate-smart agriculture — seasonal planning, weather monitoring, and adaptive farming techniques.

🥗 Nutritional Programs

Supporting food banks and school feeding programs during drought seasons to ensure children and vulnerable families don't go hungry.

Program 05

⛪ Faith-Based Community Coalitions

Churches, mosques, and faith communities are the most trusted institutions in Kamba society. ADADO works hand-in-hand with faith leaders in North Carolina and Kenya to mobilize resources, spread community awareness, and deliver programs to the grassroots.

  • Partnering with Kamba churches in North Carolina for fundraising and community events
  • Supporting faith-led community development projects in Machakos, Kitui, and Makueni
  • Building interfaith bridges that unite all Kamba community members around shared development goals
Program 06

🏛️ Town Halls & Community Forums

We believe in radical inclusion. Every Kamba voice — young and old, home and abroad — deserves to shape our community's direction. ADADO hosts regular town halls in North Carolina and virtual forums connecting the diaspora with Kenya.

  • Quarterly in-person community meetings in North Carolina
  • Annual diaspora-Kenya virtual summits bringing the community together
  • Community polls, surveys, and open forums to guide ADADO's agenda democratically
